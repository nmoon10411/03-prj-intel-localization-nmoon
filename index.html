Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey towards a greener future with innovation and responsibility.

1968 — Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, starting decades of innovation.

1971 — First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution.

1978 — 8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that drives modern PCs and servers.

1985 — 386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 with 32-bit architecture, enabling major leaps in performance and multitasking.

2006 — Peak GHG Emissions

Intel reaches peak operational emissions and accelerates investments in abatement and renewables.

2020 — RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE strategy, focusing on responsibility, inclusivity, sustainability, and enabling.

2022 — Net-Zero by 2040

Commitment to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 & 2) across global operations by 2040.

2023 — 99% Renewable Electricity

Intel reaches 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, cutting emissions and advancing long-term goals.

2024 — Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting leaders to collaborate on greener manufacturing.
