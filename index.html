Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, starting decades of innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel launches the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution.

1978

8086 Processor

Intel introduces the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that powers modern computing.

1985

386 Processor

The 386 processor brings 32-bit computing, boosting performance and enabling multitasking PCs.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions lead to major sustainability investments.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE strategy with ambitious 2030 climate and sustainability goals.

2022

Net-Zero by 2040

Intel commits to net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across global operations by 2040.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, cutting emissions dramatically.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting leaders to discuss eco-friendly innovation.
