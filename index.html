1968 - Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, starting decades of innovation.

1971 - First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution.

1978 - 8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that drives modern PCs and servers.

1985 - 386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, enabling advanced computing power.

2006 - Peak GHG Emissions

Intel reaches peak greenhouse gas emissions before committing to major environmental initiatives.

2020 - RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE strategy, focusing on responsibility, inclusivity, and sustainability.

2022 - Net-Zero 2040

Intel commits to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across operations by 2040.

2023 - 99% Renewable Energy

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage globally, pushing sustainability forward.

2024 - Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting leaders to discuss future innovations.
